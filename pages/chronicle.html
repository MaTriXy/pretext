SCENT OF AN IDEA:
Winding Contours

Some pages do not need CSS columns so much as a memory of where the text has already been. The fun begins when every line can steer around a shape without giving the browser the whole composition back.

A layout like this is less about imitation than leverage: one body of text, several live contours, and a surface that can be resized without collapsing into a stack of boxes. The page does not guess where its text went. It knows.

The first sketch smelled of cedar, toner, and hot dust from the projector. Someone had dragged a contour across the page, and suddenly the prose no longer wanted to live in obedient rectangles.

Resize the page and the whole field reflows in userland: same text, new anchors, no hidden browser paragraph model to measure back out after the fact.

Live userland reflow. Every body line here is placed by repeated layoutNextLine() calls around fixed copy blocks and procedural artwork.
Resize the window to recompose the field.
